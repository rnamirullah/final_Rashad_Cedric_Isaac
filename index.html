Championship Hangover


By: Rashad Amirullah, Cedric Scheu, Isaac Garcia


What is Championship Hangover?

The phrase “Championship Hangover” is a basketball reference that refers to a team winning an NBA Championship and then experiencing a significant decline in performance in the following season.


Potential Causes

  • Injuries - Teams that win a championship have the shortest offseason out of all teams. Therefore, they have the least time to recover physically.
  • Players lose the motivation to win a championship
  • Opposing coaches figure out ways to out-strategize the team
  • Teams may not be able to afford to resign key players and they end up leaving to other teams


Statistics Used

  • Wins, Losses, and Winning % - wins divided by total games
  • Winning % Differential - Difference between winning % in the following year vs. championship winning season
  • Opposing coaches figure out ways to out-strategize the team
  • Offensive and Defensive Rating - Points scored and points allowed per 100 possessions
  • Net Rating - Difference between offensive and defensive rating
  • Net Rating Rank and Diff. in Net Rating Rank - Where the team's net rating ranked among NBA teams and how that ranking changed between seasons
  • Postseason Success on a scale of 1-5 - 0 (did not qualify for playoffs), 1 (reached first round), 2 (reached second round), 3 (reached conference finals), 4 (reached finals), 5 (won finals)
